Synopsis

Use this form of the init function to initialize an arbitrary transform kernel, as recorded in an ATK marker segment. The info[0].support_length coefficients associated with the first analysis lifting step appear first in the coefficients array, followed by the info[1].support_length coefficients of the second lifting step, and so forth. In the case of reversible transforms, the floating-point coefficients must be multiplied by 2 to the power of the corresponding kdu_kernel_step_info::downshift, in order to recover the integer-valued coefficients which are actually used in a reversible implementation.
